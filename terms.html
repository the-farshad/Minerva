The hosted instance

The hosted instance at minerva.thefarshad.com is provided as a courtesy. It runs on a small DigitalOcean droplet behind Caddy and Cloudflare. It may change, slow down, go down, or be discontinued at any time. There is no service-level agreement and no support obligation. If continuity matters to you, fork the repository and host your own copy — the hosted instance and a self-hosted copy are functionally identical.

Some helper-backed features (video downloads, paper PDF mirror to host disk, CORS-proxied bibliographic lookups, PDF text extraction, Postgres mirror) require a helper service to be reachable. The hosted instance ships its own helper bound to the droplet's loopback interface; you may instead point Minerva at a helper running on your own machine. Either way, the helper is opt-in and is documented in the privacy policy.

Your data

Minerva's hosted SPA does not store your data. Your data lives in:

  • Your Google Drive (the Minerva spreadsheet plus, optionally, an Minerva offline folder for downloaded videos / mirrored paper PDFs and a minerva-config.json Settings snapshot).
  • Your browser's localStorage and IndexedDB (caches, preferences, the offline-rows mirror).
  • The host disk and (optionally) Postgres of whichever machine you run the helper on. For the hosted droplet, that machine is operated by the maintainer; see the privacy policy section "Hosted instance, custody, and access" for the specifics.

You are responsible for backing up your Google account using Google Takeout or any other tool of your choice. Minerva does not back up your data and cannot recover it.

Acceptable use

Don't use Minerva to do anything illegal in your jurisdiction or to violate Google's Terms of Service. Specifically, regarding the helper-backed features:

  • Video downloads use yt-dlp. You are responsible for having the right to download whatever you point it at — copyright in the destination video is yours to respect, not the maintainer's to enforce. Don't use the hosted instance to mass-mirror copyrighted material; abusive behaviour against YouTube or any other source is not what the hosted helper is for.
  • The CORS proxy forwards requests only to a fixed allow-list of bibliographic and YouTube hostnames. Do not attempt to bypass this allow-list.
  • The hosted helper's disk space is shared with whoever else uses the hosted instance. Avoid storing large files there if you'd be unhappy losing them; the maintainer reserves the right to wipe the helper's ~/Minerva directory at any time as part of routine ops.
  • The public-share feature embeds the data you provide into a URL anyone with the URL can read. Don't share things you wouldn't share publicly.
